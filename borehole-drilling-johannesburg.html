What You Need to Know

Gauteng is South Africa's most densely populated province and its most water-stressed. Rand Water distributes over 1.6 billion litres per day yet demand routinely exceeds supply, leaving Johannesburg businesses and households vulnerable to tariff hikes, boil-water notices, and pressure cuts. The Witwatersrand Supergroup aquifer systems — locked beneath hard granite and quartzite formations — offer a reliable underground alternative. Everest Drilling's heavy-duty rigs are engineered specifically for hard-rock penetration, across Sandton, Midrand, Centurion, Soweto, and Johannesburg South. Actual depth depends on site-specific geological conditions.

With a branch mobilising from Johannesburg, Everest Drilling offers prompt response times across the full Gauteng metro. Every project begins with a geophysical electromagnetic survey — reducing dry-borehole risk from ~40% to under 10% — followed by drilling, pump installation, and optional overhead tank construction. One contractor managing the full scope.

Borehole Drilling in Johannesburg — Key Facts

What is borehole drilling in Johannesburg?

Borehole drilling in Johannesburg involves drilling a narrow vertical shaft into the ground to access underground water-bearing aquifers beneath the Gauteng region. Drilling penetrates Hard granite & quartzite formations typical of the Johannesburg area. A completed borehole is fitted with a submersible or hand pump, optional overhead tank, and pipework to connect the borehole to the property's water distribution system.
