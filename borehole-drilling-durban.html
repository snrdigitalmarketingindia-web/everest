Borehole Drilling in Durban — Key Facts

What is borehole drilling in Durban?

Borehole drilling in Durban involves drilling a narrow vertical shaft into the ground to access underground water-bearing aquifers beneath the KwaZulu-Natal region. Drilling penetrates Coastal sedimentary & dolerite formations typical of the KwaZulu-Natal region. A completed borehole is fitted with a submersible or hand pump, optional overhead tank, and pipework to connect the borehole to the property's water distribution system.

How deep are boreholes in Durban?
Borehole depth in Durban and KwaZulu-Natal varies significantly depending on the specific location, proximity to the coast, and the underlying geological formation. Coastal areas typically encounter sedimentary layers, while inland KZN has dolerite intrusions. A geophysical survey is the most reliable way to estimate required depth for your site.
