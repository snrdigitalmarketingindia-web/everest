Pump Systems April 2026 · 5 min

Submersible Pumps vs Hand Pumps: Which Is Right for Your Borehole?

Submersible pumps are installed below water level inside the borehole casing and deliver pressurised water to your property automatically. Hand pumps are manually operated, require no electricity, and suit remote or backup applications. Choosing the right pump depends on your application, location, and infrastructure. Everest installs and commissions both types.

Water Storage March 2026 · 5 min

How Does an Overhead Water Tank Work?

An overhead tank stores water at elevation, allowing gravity to deliver consistent pressure to taps, toilets, and irrigation systems without requiring a continuously running pump. When paired with a borehole submersible pump, an elevated tank creates a reliable, pressure-fed water system that operates independently of municipal supply and Eskom.

Common Borehole Problems and What Causes Them

Reduced flow, pump noise, air discharge, and silty water are among the most common borehole issues South African property owners encounter. Most problems have identifiable causes — worn pump impellers, borehole siltation, casing damage, or pump sizing mismatches. Understanding the cause is the first step to the right solution.

Why a Geophysical Survey Saves You Money Before Drilling

Drilling without a survey risks a dry or low-yielding borehole. A geophysical survey maps underground fractures and aquifer zones using electromagnetic technology before a single metre is drilled — identifying the optimal location on your property. It is the most cost-effective risk reduction step in any borehole project.

A correctly installed borehole with a properly sized pump and regular maintenance can provide reliable water supply for decades.
