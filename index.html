How Deep Should a Borehole Be in South Africa?

Borehole depth in South Africa varies by region and geology. In the Eastern Cape, most boreholes reach water between 40–120m. Gauteng and KZN typically require 80–180m. Our TATA Prima rig drills to 250m, covering virtually any formation.

5 Signs Your Property Needs a Borehole

High municipal water bills, unreliable supply, agricultural demand, new construction or water restrictions are all strong indicators that a private borehole will pay for itself within 2–3 years. Call us for a free evaluation.

How to Maintain Your Borehole Pump for 10+ Years

A submersible pump correctly installed and maintained can run for over a decade. Key steps: annual water testing, monitor flow rate changes, protect the borehole casing and service the pump every 2 years. Everest offers maintenance contracts.

Borehole Drilling Costs in South Africa: 2026 Price Guide

Drilling costs range from R500–R1,200 per metre depending on formation hardness and location. A complete 100m borehole with pump and tank typically costs R60,000–R150,000 — far less than years of municipal water tariffs. Everest offers competitive pricing.

Geophysics
January 2026 · 5 min read

Why a Geophysical Survey Saves You Money Before Drilling

Drilling in the wrong location is expensive — a dry borehole still costs R50,000+. A geophysical survey uses electromagnetic scanning to identify water-bearing fractures, increasing success rates from 60% to over 90%. Always survey first.

Borehole Regulations in South Africa: What You Must Know

Under the National Water Act, boreholes used for domestic or agricultural purposes must be registered with the Department of Water and Sanitation. Everest Drilling assists clients with all compliance paperwork and water use licence applications.
